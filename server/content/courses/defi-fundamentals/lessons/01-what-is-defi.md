# Lesson 1 — What is DeFi? (Theory)

## Summary

**DeFi** (Decentralized Finance) is a set of financial applications built on
open blockchain networks. Instead of relying on traditional intermediaries, DeFi
uses smart contracts and open protocols to enable things like trading, lending,
saving, and payments.

## Learning objectives

- Define DeFi and how it differs from traditional finance
- Identify common DeFi primitives (tokens, exchanges, lending)
- Understand the main benefits and risks of DeFi

## DeFi in one sentence

DeFi is **software that behaves like financial infrastructure**: rules are
encoded in contracts, and anyone can interact with those rules if they meet the
requirements.

## Common DeFi primitives

### Tokens

Tokens represent value on-chain (stablecoins, governance tokens, LP tokens,
etc.). They can be transferred, held in wallets, and used inside protocols.

### Exchanges

Users trade assets via:

- Order books (buyers/sellers place offers)
- Automated market makers (AMMs) with liquidity pools

### Lending and borrowing

Protocols let users:

- Deposit assets to earn yield
- Borrow against collateral

### Oracles

Oracles provide external data (like prices). Many DeFi protocols depend on price
feeds to manage collateral and liquidations.

## Why DeFi is powerful

- **Self-custody**: you can hold your own assets
- **Composability**: protocols can be combined like “money legos”
- **Transparency**: rules and state are often publicly auditable

## The risks (be honest)

- Smart contract bugs
- Economic attacks (bad incentives, oracle manipulation)
- Liquidity risks (slippage, thin markets)
- User error (phishing, approvals to malicious contracts)
